About TIRRA

Get to know the Thetis Island Residents and Ratepayers Association

Mission & Purpose

TIRRA advocates on behalf of island residents and property owners, working with regional and provincial partners to address issues affecting our community. We manage and coordinate key services, promote community welfare and support local initiatives that enhance life on Thetis Island.

As a volunteer‑run organization, our success depends on the active participation of our members. We encourage all residents and property owners to become members and join us in shaping the future of our island.

Membership

Membership in TIRRA is open to all Thetis Island residents and ratepayers. Joining gives you a voice in community decisions, supports essential services and helps fund local projects. Annual dues are modest and go directly towards operating and maintaining services such as the recycle yard, trails and cemetery.

To become a member, please visit our Membership page to apply online or download the current membership form. Paper forms remain available at community meetings on request.

Board & Governance

TIRRA is governed by a Board of Directors elected by members at our annual general meeting. Directors volunteer their time to oversee our services, manage finances and represent the interests of the community. Board positions include President, Vice President, Secretary, Treasurer and committee chairs for services. A current list of directors and contact information will be posted here following each AGM.

History

Thetis Island residents have organized to support community services for decades. TIRRA was formally incorporated to provide a unified voice for residents and to manage responsibilities such as the solid waste program and trails. We honour the work of islanders like Veronica Shelford, who created the original Thetis website, and many other volunteers whose dedication has shaped our community.
